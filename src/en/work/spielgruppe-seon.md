brief:
  heading: The brief
  body: |
    The Spielgruppe Seon needed a website — not just a page, but a real online presence that parents could trust and find easily. The goal was simple: give families the information they need to decide if the group is right for their child, and make it easy to get in touch.

    There was no existing site, no brand guidelines, and no template to start from. Just a need and a deadline.
problem:
  heading: The problem
  body: |
    Parents looking for a local playgroup rely heavily on first impressions. A missing or poorly structured website signals disorganisation — and for a group working with young children, trust is everything.

    The challenge was to create a site that felt warm and welcoming without being unprofessional, and that answered the most important questions (What happens there? When? For whom? How do I register?) without overwhelming new visitors.
insightQuote: "For parents of young children, trust is the product. The website either builds it in the first ten seconds or loses it."
insightAttr: Core insight
principles:
  heading: Design priorities
  items:
    - number: "1"
      title: Answer the key questions immediately
      body: Parents scan, they do not read. The most important information — age range, schedule, location, registration — had to be visible without scrolling.
    - number: "2"
      title: Warm, not childish
      body: The visual tone had to appeal to parents, not children. Trustworthy, friendly, and clean rather than busy or cartoon-like.
    - number: "3"
      title: Low maintenance for the team
      body: The group is run by volunteers. The site had to be simple enough that non-technical people could update it without my help.
process:
  heading: How it came together
  items:
    - number: "01"
      title: Understand the real audience
      body: The users were not the playgroup staff — they were parents, most of them new to the area or new to the concept of a Spielgruppe. I started by mapping out what they would want to know and in what order.
    - number: "02"
      title: Define the information architecture
      body: I structured the content around the key parent questions — what the group offers, who it is for, when and where it meets, and how to register. Simple, linear, no dead ends.
    - number: "03"
      title: Design and build
      body: I designed the layout in Figma first, then built the site. I kept the visual language clean and warm — readable typography, soft colours, clear hierarchy — so the content could do its job without the design getting in the way.
outcomes:
  heading: Result
  metrics:
    - value: "0 → live"
      label: From no website to full online presence
    - value: Simple
      label: Easy for non-technical volunteers to maintain
    - value: Real users
      label: Designed for parents, tested with parents
reflection:
  pull: A small project with real stakes is still a real project.
  body: |
    Spielgruppe Seon was my first end-to-end design and build project for a real client with real users. It taught me that even a simple brief requires genuine research — because the users' needs and the client's needs are rarely the same thing.

    It also confirmed that constraints (no budget, no brand, no existing content) are not obstacles — they are the brief.
